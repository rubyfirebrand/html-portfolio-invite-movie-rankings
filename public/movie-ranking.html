The Best Movies According to Christine

My top movies of all time (not in order).

Crazy Rich Asians

This film has some refreshing representation of Chinese Americans and their related family perspectives. I love how this movie achieved being a fun romcom while also having the main character overcome a struggle of selfworth and confidence. These struggles were overcome without the main character winning the romantic interest or the romantic interest saving the main character.

Ever After

This is a historical romance classic. We get to see the main character thrive during conversations of higher learing. Her concern for lower and upper classes provides a space for philosophical queries that can still apply today. This is also another great example of a main character resolving most of her issues without the assistance of the romantic interest.

Old Guard

This movie felt monumental in the fact that the women in this movie were never sexualized. It was also monumental because of its representation that included main characters of multiple nationalities as well as a developed LGBGTQ+ relationship.

The Lord of the Rings series

I doubt any book to movie series reaches this level of accuracy. While many people will rank Game of Thrones as one of the best fantasy series, it still lacks a quality that Lord of the Rings does not - Women main characters being classicly sexualized. The Lord of the Rings shows that you can still have political intrigue, high risk, many countries involved while not bending to the desire to show women getting sexualized, raped, and mutilated.

I will say both of these movies fall short in properly showing represenatation. The original Lord of the Rings series has the typical dark and light skin archetypes. Game of Thrones does show a wider variety of people, but those of darker skin are often considered "exotic" or "barbaric".

The Dressmaker

The cinematography in this movie is absolutely gorgeous! I love the way that we can view contrasts between the past and present through color and camera perspective. The characters have been developed in such a unqiue way! Again, this move demonstrates that a woman main character does not need a man to solve their problems.

My Name

My reason for loving this series is similar to why I love Old Guard. The main character is never sexualized. The fighting is messy and portrayed very similarly to a typical violent masculine lead film. This is another series that typifies that the woman main character will not need a man to resolve their iessues. My one concern with this series is that I don't think it would pass the Bechdel test.
